I won 1st runner-up at the Vyrothon Hackathon 2026, hosted at NSTP NUST.

Competing against 500+ participants from across Pakistan, I ranked among the top 10 individual participants, and the project won a $1,000 prize.

## Stage One: grabpic

In the first stage, I developed **grabpic**, a high-performance image processing backend designed for large-scale events. The architecture is engineered for high concurrency and efficient metadata extraction to address the challenges of real-time image distribution.

By optimizing the processing pipeline, the system supports rapid indexing and delivery of visual assets while maintaining low latency.

Project link: [grabpic](https://lnkd.in/dz_WiNgx)

## Stage Two: see-with-me

During the second stage, the project evolved into **see-with-me**, an MVP focused on collaborative visual intelligence. The platform architecture uses real-time data streams and low-latency synchronization to provide a shared visual context.

The solution aims to translate complex environments into actionable data, providing a scalable tool for remote assistance and accessibility.

Project link: [see-with-me](https://lnkd.in/d4uNETUk)

The event was a valuable opportunity for rapid prototyping and technical execution. I appreciate the Vyro team and Google Developer Groups on Campus Air University team for organizing a professional and challenging competition.
